Michel Campan

Partner - President

A graduate of the French Fashion Institute (IFM), he began his career working for large communications groups such as DDB and Altedia. In 1996, he founded one of France’s first Internet agencies.

Between 2000 and 2006, he set up the Hermès e-commerce network in the U.S., in Europe and in Japan. He then joined the L’Oréal Group to manage Internet and CRM strategy for Lancôme. In this role, he launched the first ever online sales website for a luxury brand in China.
He was named “E-commerce Man of the Year” by E-commerce magazine in 2008. In the same year, he joined Christian Dior Couture to launch the first “branded content” operation for a house of haute couture: “The Lady Noire Affair”, a film directed by Olivier Dahan and featuring Marion Cotillard. In 2009, he founded "SAME SAME but different".

Julien Gaubert-Molina

Associate Executive Director

A graduate of Léonard de Vinci University in Paris, Julien started his career with IBM, first in France and subsequently in London, where he developed skills in operational marketing. He then worked with Hermès in both New York and Paris, participating in the development of Hermès.com and of the websites of the John Lobb and Puiforcat brands.

During this time, he developed his digital skills as a project manager, participating in the re-designing of the purchasing process for Hermès.com and acquiring product sensitivity while coordinating production photography and editorial updates for the various Hermès websites.
In June 2009, Julien Gaubert-Molina joined the Leather Goods Division of Hermès as marketing manager with responsibility for the commercial and operational development of the collections. He participated in the coordination of collection purchases and sales promotion, establishing common Visual Merchandising standards for Hermès boutiques.
He joined "SAME SAME but different" in 2012.

Victor Yang

Partner - Client development and management

Between 2004 & 2010, Victor spent 6 years in L’Oréal China as Luxury Products Director. In 2006, he launched the first luxury cosmetic community in China for Lancôme, Rosebeauty (www.rosebeauty.com.cn), which gathered more than 600,000 active fans of Lancôme. In 2007, he created the first B2C e-commerce website of Lancôme (www.lancome.com.cn). From 2007 to 2010, the e-commerce Lancôme website’s growth rate reached 110% and the 2010 revenue was around 5 million euros. After this success, Victor also launched others e-commerce website for L’Oréal as Biotherm, Kiehl’s, Giorgio Armani Beauty and Shu Uemura. In 2010, he started LuxuryBeauty (www.luxurybeauty.com), which is the L’Oréal first multi-brands e-commerce website worldwide. In 2009, Lancôme was ranked by L2thinktank (www.l2thinktank.com) first brand out of the 100 luxury brands in China in terms of digital IQ. In 2010, Victor joined SAME SAME AGENCY and created the subsidiary company, SAME SAME China.

 victor@samesamechina.com

Frédéric Boudet

Partner - Client development and management

After a Master’s degree in communication from the CELSA-Sorbonne School of Journalism, Frédéric Boudet started his career as a journalist in print media. Having spent nine years as an associate consultant at Proway, a recruitment agency specialising in advertising and the media, he joined Bouygues Telecom in 2000 as Section Editor for Current Affairs & Multimedia on the services and contents portal www.6sens.com. He then worked as Internet and E-commerce Communications Manager for Bouygues before leaving in 2009 to found "SAME SAME but different". He is also an author whose work is published by Editions de l’Olivier.

Mikaïl Osmany

Partner - Strategic planning and creation

After a Master’s degree in political science and postgraduate studies at the French Press Institute (IFP), Mikaïl Osmany started his career as Deputy Editor on the editorial team covering the French challenge for the America’s Cup in 2000 in New Zealand.  He then joined Bouygues Telecom as Editor of www.6sens.com, the telecom operator’s services and contents portal. He participated in the launching of Match TV before creating Pataquès, an agency specialising in editorial communication and the free press. He then joined the Nextedia group to develop its strategic planning. In 2009, he founded "SAME SAME but different".
